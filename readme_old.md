## Work culture
### General
- [1 min | What I Wish Someone Had Told Me | Sam Altman](https://blog.samaltman.com/what-i-wish-someone-had-told-me) - Some notes about leading an organization.
- [3 min | It matters who does science | Holden Thorp](https://www.science.org/content/blog-post/it-matters-who-does-science) - About seeing scientists as people and diversity in science, by the Editor-in-Chief of Science journals. 
- [5 min | The Tao of Cheese | Iain Cheeseman (Twitter thread)](https://twitter-thread.com/t/1432654418437095431) - Words of wisdom for how to navigate science and your career.
- [8 min | How to create a lab-group logo that stands out from the crowd | Tay et al.](https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-023-02358-2) - An eye-catching logo says a lot about your lab’s research, workplace culture and collaborative potential. Take time to get it right.
- [9 min | Health tips for research groups | Norris et al.](https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-018-05146-5) - Nature asked scientists to recommend one thing that institutional and laboratory leaders could do to make science more productive, rigorous and happy.
- [10 min | How lab heads can learn to lead | Roberta Kwok](https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-018-05156-3) - Leadership experts translate business and coaching principles into advice for PIs on running healthier, more effective and supportive research groups.
- [11 min | The Thrill of Defeat | Bob Goldstein](https://web.archive.org/web/20240914025143/https://nautil.us/the-thrill-of-defeat-235312/) - What the stories of Francis Crick and Sydney Brenner can teach us about being "scooped" and open science.
- [16 min | Ten simple rules towards healthier research labs | Fernando Maestre](https://journals.plos.org/ploscompbiol/article?id=10.1371/journal.pcbi.1006914) - 10 simple rules to make the working environment of research labs more nurturing, collaborative, and people-centered.
- [16 min | Ten simple rules to cultivate belonging in collaborative data science research teams | Gaynor et al.](https://journals.plos.org/ploscompbiol/article?id=10.1371/journal.pcbi.1010567) - Ideas to increase inclusiveness and belonging in teams (in data science, but applies more generally).
- [20 min | Ten simple rules to improve academic work–life balance | Bartlett et al.](https://journals.plos.org/ploscompbiol/article?id=10.1371/journal.pcbi.1009124) - How to improve ones own well-being, and how environments can promote well-being and so trainees can thrive and move forward in their careers.
- [20 min | A field guide to cultivating computational biology | Way et al.](https://journals.plos.org/plosbiology/article?id=10.1371/journal.pbio.3001419) - Biology in the digital era requires computation and collaboration. We propose a general solution: “deep integration” between biology and the computational sciences.
- [22 min | Science, narcissism and the quest for visibility | Bruno Lemaitre](https://febs.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1111/febs.14032) - About narcissistic personalities in science.
- [22 min | One hundred research rules of the game | Niamh Brennan (.pdf)](https://www.emerald.com/aaaj/article-pdf/32/2/691/11751/aaaj-02-2019-032.pdf)[ (page)](https://www.emerald.com/insight/content/doi/10.1108/AAAJ-02-2019-032/full/html) - 100 "rules" for making your research "world class".
- [>60 min | Collection: How to grow a healthy lab | Nature](https://www.nature.com/collections/pmlcrkkyyq) - Collection of articles for a healthy research environment.

### Lab manual
- [4 min | The key to a happy lab life is in the manual | Mariam Aly](https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-018-06167-w) - A well-crafted lab manual with guidelines and advice can save time, reassure trainees and promote a positive lab culture.
- [5 min | How to write a lab handbook | Samuel Mehr](https://thebiologist.rsb.org.uk/biologist-features/how-to-write-a-lab-handbook)[meta handbook (.pdf)](https://thebiologist.rsb.org.uk/images/biologist/2020/Apr_May_2020_67-2/67.2_Handbook.pdf) - How a good lab handbook can improve your team's science and save time. Includes a meta handbook with twenty common topics. 
- [5 min | Inside the Duolingo Company Handbook](https://blog.duolingo.com/handbook/) - Strategies behind how Duolingo builts their company, culture, and brand. Key principles and how they apply them to hiring, design, engineering...
- [10 min | Research Culture: Why every lab needs a handbook | Tenndler et al.](https://elifesciences.org/articles/88853) - A good lab handbook will benefit all the members of a research group, by explaining how a lab works, outlining what is expected of everyone, and describing the culture the lab is aiming for.

### Mentoring
- [1 min | Features of good mentors | Petr Svoboda (Twitter thread)](https://twitter-thread.com/t/1828578473612603840) - Aspects to consider in good mentoring. (1 min read)
- [3 min | How I learned to nurture relationships in academia—and be more kind | Roel Snieder](https://www.science.org/content/article/how-i-learned-to-nurture-relationships-in-academia-be-more-kind) - How a different mindset can benefit everyone.
- [5 min | Mentorship Malpractice | Chopra, Edelson, Saint](https://faculty.uwmedicine.org/wp-content/uploads/2024/09/Mentorship-Malpractice-JAMA-2016.pdf) - Some stereotypes in bad mentorship and possible solutions to handle these. 
- [5 min | Why scientific criticism sometimes needs to hurt | Daniel Lakens](https://daniellakens.blogspot.com/2016/09/why-scientific-criticism-sometimes.html) - About the value of getting criticism (even if it sometimes hurts). 
- [6 min | What’s a failed experiment? | Sophien Kamoun](https://kamounlab.medium.com/whats-a-failed-experiment-7ea66fd96f8) - On experimental design and how to handle hypothesis-falsifying or inconclusive results. 
- [8 min | Landis Mentoring Award Letter | Bernardo Sabatini](https://github.com/bernardosabatini/writings/blob/main/Sabatini%20Landis%20Award.pdf) - Thoughts on which principles could help to be a good mentor. 
- [9 min | On being an advisor to today’s junior scientists | David Allis](https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.1704511114) - How advisors can help team members in their training and education.
- [18 min | Ten Simple Rules to becoming a principal investigator | Tregoning, McDermott](https://journals.plos.org/ploscompbiol/article?id=10.1371/journal.pcbi.1007448) - Ten tricky steps on becoming a research group leader. 
- [19 min | The Making of a Scientist | Hans Krebs, 1967](https://doi.org/10.1038/2151441a0) - A nobel prize awardee on the importance of training/mentorship, explaining the "scientific genealogy" of awardee networks. 
- [20 min | Precision & Plasticity in Mentoring | Angela DePace](https://youtu.be/rTYjbv9zmB0) - Normalizing failure, leading by example, using conflict-management tools, and yearly development meetings. So trainees can thrive as whole people and stay in science. 

### Finding good projects
- [1 min | Thoughts on learning to "fail fast" in research | Shengwu Li (Twitter thread)](https://twitter-thread.com/t/1385614637970886658) - Why one should prioritize tasks that can quickly reveal that a project is not worthwhile. 
- [3 min | Kill Ideas Quickly | Chris Gregg](https://web.archive.org/web/20230314222643/https://gregglab.neuro.utah.edu/2018/10/01/kill-ideas-quickly-natural-selection-is-why-you-need-to-work-so-hard-as-a-scientist/) - Thoughts on quickly sorting out research ideas.
- [4 min | The importance of stupidity in scientific research | Martin Schwartz](https://jcs.biologists.org/content/121/11/1771) - Essay on "feeling stupid" when doing research.
- [5 min | Make science disruptive again | Yanai, Lercher](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41587-023-01736-5) - Advocating to include idea-finding and creativity into graduate student programs. 
- [8 min | How to choose a good scientific problem | Uri Alon](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19782018) - Choosing good problems is essential for being a good scientist. But what is a good problem, and how do you choose one? 
- [13 min | Theory in Biology: Figure 1 or Figure 7? | Rob Phillips](https://www.cell.com/trends/cell-biology/fulltext/S0962-8924(15)00194-4) - How theory allows us to sharpen our thinking and hypotheses.
- [16 min | Philosophy of Biology: The analysis of living systems can generate both knowledge and illusions | Antony Jose](https://elifesciences.org/articles/56354) - Thinking about experimental techniques in six broad approaches – perturbation, visualization, substitution, characterization, reconstitution, and simulation.
- [13/9/4 min | Modest Advice for... | Stephen Stearns, 1987](https://stearnslab.yale.edu/modest-advice)  /  [Acynical advice for... | Raymond Huey, 1987](https://esajournals.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/pdf/10.2307/20166581)  /  [Some Pragmatic Advice to... | Brian Witz, 1994](https://www.kevintshoemaker.net/pdf/Witz_grad_advice.pdf) - ...graduate students. Three articles that still contain some truths and otherwise gives insight into the thinking of older generations of scientists.
- [>60 min | Night Science articles in Genome Biology | Yanai, Lercher](https://www.biomedcentral.com/collections/night-science) - "Night science is where we explore the unstructured realm of possible hypotheses, of ideas not yet fully fleshed out".
- [>60 min | Night Science Podcast series | Yanai, Lercher](https://nightscience.buzzsprout.com/1744020) - Where do ideas come from? Each episode explores science's creative side with a leading colleague.

## Data visualization
### General
- [3 min | Axes, ticks and grids | Martin Krzywinski](https://www.nature.com/articles/nmeth.2337) - Make navigational elements distinct and unobtrusive to maintain visual priority of data.
- [3 min | Negative space | Bang Wong ](https://www.nature.com/articles/nmeth0111-5) - About negative space and how to use it to improve visual structure.
- [5 min | Guide to designing conceptual figures | Nature Reviews (.pdf)](https://www.nature.com/documents/natrev-artworkguide.pdf) - Guiding principles for
conceptual figures or explanatory visualizations. 
- [5-15 min | EMBO Press Figure Guidelines | EMBOpress (.pdf)](https://www.embopress.org/pb-assets/embo-site/EMBOPress_Figure_Guidelines_061115.pdf) - Technical requirements and layout dimensions for figures in the EMBO Press titles.
- [5 min | Nature Figure Guidelines | Nature (.pdf)](https://www.nature.com/documents/Final_guide_to_authors.pdf) - Technical requirements and layout dimensions for figures in Nature.
- [6 min | Same Stats, Different Graphs | Matejka, Fitzmaurice](https://www.autodesk.com/research/publications/same-stats-different-graphs) - Illustrations of how summary statistics can have wildly different underlying graphs and data distributions.
- [8 min | The graphics principles cheat sheet | Graphics Principles team, Novartis (.pdf)](https://github.com/GraphicsPrinciples/CheatSheet/blob/master/NVSCheatSheet.pdf)[(page)](https://graphicsprinciples.github.io/cheatsheet.html) - A single-page reference sheet providing detailed practical recommendations for creating effective graphs.
- [13 min | Friends don't let friends make certain types of data visualization | Chenxin Li](https://github.com/cxli233/FriendsDontLetFriends) - Which plots to avoid and how to plot it instead. 
- [17 min | Ten Simple Rules for Better Figures | Rougier, Droettboom, Bourne](https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pcbi.1003833) - Basic set of rules to improve figure design and to explain some of the common pitfalls.
- [17 min | Ten simple rules for designing graphical abstracts | Jambor, Bornhäuser](https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pcbi.1011789) - Advice on designing graphical abstracts, much also applies more generally to explanatory visualizations.
- [19 min | Reveal, Don’t Conceal | Weissgerber et al.](https://doi.org/10.1161/CIRCULATIONAHA.118.037777) - Primer to improve data visualization in publications, with some examples of step-by-step improvements.
- [19 min | Choosing Suitable Color Palettes for Accessible and Accurate Science Figures | Crameri, Shepard, Heron](https://doi.org/10.1002/cpz1.1126) - How to choose colors for data visualizations. 
- [22 min | Community-developed checklists for publishing images and image analyses | Schmied et al.](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41592-023-01987-9) - Checklists for preparing light microscopy images, describing image analyses with recommendations for image formatting, annotation, and color selection.
- [(collection) >60 min | Blog helenajamborwrites | Helena Jambor](https://helenajamborwrites.netlify.app/) - A collection of how-to's on data visualization and microscope images.
- [(book) >60 min  min | Fundamentals of Data Visualization | Claus Wilke](https://clauswilke.com/dataviz/index.html) - A guide to making visualizations that accurately reflect the data, tell a story, and look professional. (full book)
- [(tool) | Color Oracle Software | Bernie Jenny](https://colororacle.org/) - Free color blindness simulator for Windows, Mac and Linux
- [(tool) | Find the graphic you need | Data to Viz team](https://www.data-to-viz.com/) - Classification of all possible chart types classified following the input data format.
- [(tool) | The Data Visualisation Catalogue | Severino Ribecca](https://datavizcatalogue.com/) - Guide and library of different data visualization techniques.

### Specific plots

- [Heatmaps | 3 min | Dos and don’ts for a heatmap color scale | BioTuring's Blog](https://web.archive.org/web/20210620092420/https://blog.bioturing.com/2018/09/24/heatmap-color-scale/) - Brief advice on color scales of heatmaps. 
- [SuperPlots | 11 min | SuperPlots: Communicating reproducibility and variability in cell biology | Lord et al.](https://doi.org/10.1083/jcb.202001064) - Distribution–reproducibility “SuperPlots” that display the distribution of the entire dataset, and report statistics such as means, error bars, and P values (addressing the reproducibility of the findings).
- [Estimation plots | 4 min | Moving beyond P values: data analysis with estimation graphics | Ho et al.](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41592-019-0470-3) - A visual, intuitive and widely accessible tool could finally help us move from asking “does it?” to “how much?”
- [Estimation plots | 5 min | What is estimation?](https://www.estimationstats.com/#/background) - Why and how to construct estimation plots.
