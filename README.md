## Career
### Career paths
- [The World’s Most Comprehensive Resource for Scientists Transitioning to Industry | Science](http://sciencecareers.sciencemag.org/career_magazine/previous_issues/articles/2014_07_03/caredit.a1400170) - Collection of articles on transitioning from academia to industry, covering for instance: if you should, application, interviewing, salary negotiation, and expectations. 
- [Career Options for Scientists](http://cshperspectives.cshlp.org/content/9/9/a032755.full) - Short article on career choices outside of academia, by Richard Sever and Kaaren Janssen 
- [What your PI forgot to tell you: why you actually might want a job running a research lab](http://www.molbiolcell.org/content/28/13/1724.full) - Why being a PI can be a desirable job, conversation between two group leaders Amy Gladfelter and Mark Peifer.
- [Job-Seq: The Lowest Throughput Sequencing Technology](https://olgabotvinnik.com/blog/job-seq-the-lowest-throughput-sequencing-technology/) - Blog post about the job search after PhD. 
- [Postdocs’ advice on pursuing a research career in academia: A qualitative analysis of free-text survey responses](https://journals.plos.org/plosone/article?id=10.1371/journal.pone.0250662) - Summary of ~1,000 postdoc survey on "Life in Academia and Strategies for Success", US-centric. 
- [What’s it like working in biotech | Mark Charbonneau (Twitter thread)](https://twitter-thread.com/t/1398329690268577801) - Some observations on the industry and differences to academia. 

### CV/Resume
- [CVs | MIT Careers](https://capd.mit.edu/resources/cvs/) - CV format, sections and headings.

- [CV/Resume | MIT CommKit NSE](https://mitcommlab.mit.edu/nse/commkit/cvresume/) - Advice on CV/resume.
- [CV/Resume parser | OpenResume](https://www.open-resume.com/resume-parser/) - Resume parser to check how well your CV/resume will be recognized by automatic systems. Also a free, open-source, resume builder.
- [Tricks to maintain & share a CV with Google Docs | Carpenter-Singh Lab](https://carpenter-singh-lab.broadinstitute.org/blog/tricks-for-maintaining-your-cv-resume-with-google-docs-easy-to-edit-immediately-published) - Tricks for maintaining a shareable CV/Resume with Google Docs.
- [Resumes | MIT Careers](https://capd.mit.edu/resources/cvs/) - Advice on how to demonstrate your skills and achievements.
- [Rules of the Academic CV | The Professor Is In](https://theprofessorisin.com/2016/08/19/dr-karens-rules-of-the-academic-cv/) - Expected content of an academic CV. 
- Design: This is a topic on its own. It should be visually appealing to help guide the reader and organize the information. There are plenty of templates out there. One can design and produce a shareable .pdf file with different tools, for instance Word, Google Docs, which makes it easy to update continually. One can also work more graphically with Illustrator, InDesign, or programmatically with R, Latex, CSS, html. Additionally there are templates for personal websites hosted as a free page on Github.

## Culture
### General
- [Science, narcissism and the quest for visibility | Bruno Lemaitre](https://febs.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1111/febs.14032) - About narcissistic personalities in science.
- [Handbook | The key to a happy lab life is in the manual | Mariam Aly](https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-018-06167-w) - A well-crafted lab manual with guidelines and advice can save time, reassure trainees and promote a positive lab culture.
- [The Thrill of Defeat | Bob Goldstein](https://nautil.us/the-thrill-of-defeat-235312/) - What the stories of Francis Crick and Sydney Brenner can teach us about being "scooped".
- [Ten simple rules towards healthier research labs | Fernando T. Maestre](https://journals.plos.org/ploscompbiol/article?id=10.1371/journal.pcbi.1006914) - 10 simple rules to make the working environment of research labs more nurturing, collaborative, and people-centered.
- [One hundred research rules of the game | Niamh Brennan (.pdf)](https://www.emerald.com/aaaj/article-pdf/32/2/691/11751/aaaj-02-2019-032.pdf)[ (page)](https://www.emerald.com/insight/content/doi/10.1108/AAAJ-02-2019-032/full/html) - 100 "rules" for making your research "world class".
- [Health tips for research groups | Norris, Dirnagl, Zigmond, Thompson-Peer, Chow](https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-018-05146-5) - Nature asked scientists to recommend one thing that institutional and laboratory leaders could do to make science more productive, rigorous and happy.
- [Ten simple rules to improve academic work–life balance | Bartlett, Nur Arslan, Bankston, Sarabipour](https://journals.plos.org/ploscompbiol/article?id=10.1371/journal.pcbi.1009124) - How to improve ones own well-being, and how environments can promote well-being and so trainees can thrive and move forward in their careers.
- [A field guide to cultivating computational biology | Way et al.](https://journals.plos.org/plosbiology/article?id=10.1371/journal.pbio.3001419) - Biology in the digital era requires computation and collaboration. We propose a general solution: “deep integration” between biology and the computational sciences.
- [Ten simple rules to cultivate belonging in collaborative data science research teams | Gaynor et al.](https://journals.plos.org/ploscompbiol/article?id=10.1371/journal.pcbi.1010567) - Ideas to increase inclusiveness and belonging in teams (in data science, but applies more general).
- [It matters who does science | Holden Torp](https://www.science.org/content/blog-post/it-matters-who-does-science) - About seeing scientists as people and diversity in science, by the Editor-in-Chief of Science journals. 
- [Handbook | Research Culture: Why every lab needs a handbook | Tenndler et al.](https://elifesciences.org/articles/88853) - A good lab handbook will benefit all the members of a research group, by explaining how a lab works, outlining what is expected of everyone, and describing the culture the lab is aiming for.
- [How to create a lab-group logo that stands out from the crowd | Tay et al.](https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-023-02358-2) - An eye-catching logo says a lot about your lab’s research, workplace culture and collaborative potential. Take time to get it right.
- [What I Wish Someone Had Told Me | Sam Altman](https://blog.samaltman.com/what-i-wish-someone-had-told-me) - Some inspirational notes about leading an organization.
- [Handbook | How to write a lab handbook | Samuel Mehr](https://thebiologist.rsb.org.uk/biologist-features/how-to-write-a-lab-handbook)[meta handbook (.pdf)](https://thebiologist.rsb.org.uk/images/biologist/2020/Apr_May_2020_67-2/67.2_Handbook.pdf) - How a good lab handbook can improve your team's science and save time. Includes a meta handbook with twenty common topics. 
- [Handbook | Inside the Duolingo Company Handbook](https://blog.duolingo.com/handbook/) - Strategies behind how Duolingo builts their company, culture, and brand. Key principles and how they apply them to hiring, design, engineering...
- [The Tao of Cheese | Iain Cheeseman (Twitter thread)](https://twitter-thread.com/t/1432654418437095431) - Words of wisdom for how to navigate science and your career.
- [How to grow a healthy lab | Nature](https://www.nature.com/collections/pmlcrkkyyq) - Collection of articles for a healthy research environment.
