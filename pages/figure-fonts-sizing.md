# Brief advice on figures: fonts, sizing, dimensions, linewidth etc. 

---

## TL;DR: “universal” defaults

Use these settings when you design figures from scratch. They will be compatible with most high-impact biology journals and rarely need more than minor tweaks.
Work in vector graphics programs (Inkscape, Adobe Illustrator). 

- **Typeface**  
  - Use **Arial** for all labels.  
  - Use **Symbol** for Greek letters.  
  - Use **Courier** (or another monospaced font) for nucleotide / amino-acid sequences.  
  - **Embed fonts** in PDF/EPS exports.

- **Font size (at final print size)**  
  - **7 pt** for axis labels, tick labels, and legends.  
  - **≥ 6 pt** minimum anywhere (never smaller).  
  - **8 pt bold, upright** for panel letters (A, B, C or a, b, c depending on journal).  

- **Line weight**  
  - **≥ 0.5 pt** for plot lines and axes.  
  - Never go below **0.25 pt** for any line (risk of disappearing in print).  

- **Color**  
  - Work in **RGB** while designing (journals or production will convert to CMYK if needed).  

- **Figure dimensions (rough)**  
  - In very short:  
    - **Full figure:** full page width, about **half page height**.  
    - **One-column figure:** half page width, about **half page height**.

## Condensed origin

This table is *not* a full spec; it just shows why the defaults above are “safe” across families.

| Publisher / family | Typical figure fonts | Typical text size (final) | Color mode (submission) | Raster resolution (min) | Vector requirement |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Nature Portfolio** | Helvetica / Arial | 5–7 pt text; 8 pt panel labels | RGB (converted to CMYK for print) | ≥ 300 dpi photos; ≥ 1200 dpi line art | Strongly prefer editable vector for graphs |
| **Cell Press** | Arial only | ~6–8 pt text | RGB | ~300–500 dpi photos; ~1000–1200 dpi line art | Vector preferred; often ask for flattened final files |
| **Science / Science Advances** | Sans-serif (e.g. Myriad / Arial) | Legible at final size, similar to 6–8 pt | RGB, production handles print color space | ≥ 300 dpi photos; ≥ 1200 dpi line art | Vector strongly preferred for graphs |
| **EMBO Press** (EMBO J, MSB) | Helvetica / Arial, Symbol, Courier | Label height ~1.5–2.0 mm (~4–6 pt) | RGB or CMYK | ≥ 300 dpi non-vector | Vector encouraged, esp. for line art |
| **CSHL Press** (Genes & Dev, RNA) | Standard legible fonts (sans-serif for labels) | Legible at print scale (≈ ≥6 pt) | RGB/CMYK depending on production | ≥ 300 dpi photos; ≥ 1000–1200 dpi line art | Vector preferred where possible |
| **eLife** | Sans-serif labels, focus on clarity | Legible at final size (≈ ≥6–8 pt) | RGB for online; production handles print | ≥ 300 dpi photos; high-res / vector line art | Vector preferred for schematics/plots |

---

## Final reminders

- These are **design defaults**, not a replacement for each journal’s author guidelines.  
- Always **check the target journal’s current instructions for authors** before final submission.  
- If you stick to the defaults above (Arial/Helvetica, 7–8 pt text, ≥0.5 pt lines, RGB, 300/600/1200 dpi, column widths), your figures will usually need only minor tweaks when you move between journals.
